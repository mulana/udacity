White Tiger


The White Tiger (also known as the White Bengal Tiger) is a subspecies of Tiger, found throughout the Indian subcontinent.

  • Scientific Name: Panthera tigris tigris
  • Size (L): 2.4m - 3.3m (6.8ft - 11ft)
  • Average Lifespan: 10 - 20 years
  • Habitat: Dense jungle and mangrove swamp

White Bengal tigers have pink noses, white-to-creme coloured fur and black, grey or chocolate-coloured stripes. White Bengal tigers eyes are usually blue, but may be green or amber. It is a large and powerful animal that can weigh up to 300kg and reaches more than 3 meters in length. These animals are incredibly rare as their colouration is dependent on a defective, recessive gene that is passed on from their parents.
